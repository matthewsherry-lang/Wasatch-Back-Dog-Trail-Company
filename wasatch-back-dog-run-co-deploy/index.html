About the Pack

Why We Run

We work with dogs that need real output, not a quick loop around the block. Each run is a controlled conditioning session on mountain terrain, led with safety standards and pack structure.

I personally run these routes year-round with my two well-behaved dogs, and together we have logged hundreds of trail miles across the Wasatch Back, especially around Heber Valley.

Dogs must be healthy, trail-capable, and high-energy. Every outing starts with a gear check, behavior review, and route plan based on time of day, temperature, trail traffic, and season.

Safety Standards

Prepared for Terrain, Weather, and Behavior

  • First-aid trained guides carrying canine first aid kits.
  • Real-time weather checks with heat, storm, and trail closure adjustments.
  • Mandatory vaccination records and a meet-and-greet before the first run so dogs and owners feel safe and acquainted.
  • Fully insured for canine trail running and transport services.

Fit Check

Who This Is For and Not For

Great Fit

  • Healthy, high-energy dogs that need real weekly output.
  • Dogs comfortable with trail terrain, elevation, and changing weather.
  • Owners who want conditioning sessions, not a casual walk.
  • Dogs that can run safely in solo or small-group format.

Not a Fit Right Now

  • Dogs with unresolved injuries, illness, or limited mobility.
  • Puppies too young for conditioning-level trail effort.
  • Dogs needing intensive behavior rehab before pack movement.
  • Owners looking for low-intensity potty breaks or neighborhood walks.
